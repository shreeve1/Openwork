## Research Validation Workflows

### For ANY Recommendation:

#### Step 1: Initial Assessment
- Draw from expertise and experience
- Identify assumptions needing validation
- List specific data points to verify

#### Step 2: Research Validation
Execute web searches for:
- Current industry benchmarks
- Local market conditions
- Recent regulatory changes
- Competitor practices
- Cost data (materials, labor, utilities)
- Technology reviews and pricing
- Trend data from last 6 months

#### Step 3: Data Integration
- Compare findings to initial assessment
- Adjust recommendations based on current data
- Note any significant deviations
- Flag risks or opportunities discovered

#### Step 4: Presentation with Sources
- Present recommendation with confidence level
- Cite sources and dates of data
- Highlight any conflicting information
- Provide research date stamps

### Example Research Validation:

**Initial Recommendation:** "Food cost should be 30%"

**Research Validation Process:**
```
1. Search: "restaurant food cost percentage 2024"
2. Search: "food cost percentage [specific cuisine] 2024"
3. Search: "inflation impact restaurant food costs 2025"
4. Search: "[city] restaurant food prices current"
```

**Validated Recommendation:** 
"Based on current data (January 2025), food cost targets are:
- Industry average: 28-35% (source: NRA 2024 report)
- Your cuisine type: 32-38% (higher due to specialty ingredients)
- Your market: +2% adjustment for local costs
- Recommendation: Target 34% with current inflation factors"

## Research Quality Standards

### Every Research Action Must:

1. **Use Current Data**
- Prioritize 2024-2025 sources
- Flag any data older than 12 months
- Note seasonal variations

2. **Verify Multiple Sources**
- Minimum 3 sources for critical data
- Cross-reference conflicting information
- Note confidence level (High/Medium/Low)

3. **Maintain Geographic Relevance**
- Local data trumps national averages
- Account for regional variations
- Consider local regulations

4. **Document Sources**
- Include search queries used
- Note date of search
- Provide links when possible
- Flag any paywalled content
