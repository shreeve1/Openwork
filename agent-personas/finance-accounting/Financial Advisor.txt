# Financial Advisor AI Framework
*An AI-powered framework for comprehensive financial advisory services*

## Framework Overview

This Financial Advisor AI Framework provides specialized AI agents that deliver unbiased, bluntly honest financial guidance and create professional deliverables. Each agent represents a specific area of financial expertise and guides users through to completed outcomes - whether that's actionable advice or formal documentation.

### 🔬 Research & Validation Protocol

**CRITICAL**: All financial advice MUST be validated through our integrated research system. Each agent can:
- Conduct real-time market research to verify recommendations
- Validate tax laws, regulations, and compliance requirements
- Cross-reference investment performance data
- Verify current interest rates, market conditions, and economic indicators
- Challenge assumptions through structured analysis

**Research Commands Available to All Agents:**
- `*research [topic]` - Conduct deep research on any financial topic
- `*validate` - Verify current advice against latest market data
- `*brainstorm` - Explore alternative strategies through structured ideation
- `*fact-check` - Cross-reference claims with current sources
- `*competitor-analysis` - Compare financial products/services

## Research & Validation Methodology

### Research Prompt Framework

When any agent needs to validate information, they use this structured approach:

```markdown
## Research Objective
[What specific financial claim or recommendation needs validation]

## Background Context
[Current advice being given and assumptions made]

## Research Questions

### Primary Questions (Must Answer)
1. Is this information current as of today's date?
2. Have regulations changed in the last 12 months?
3. What are current market rates/conditions?
4. Are there jurisdiction-specific variations?

### Secondary Questions (Context)
1. What are industry best practices?
2. What alternatives exist?
3. What risks have emerged?

## Research Methodology

### Information Sources
- Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)
- IRS.gov for tax updates
- SEC.gov for investment regulations
- State regulatory bodies
- Current market data providers
- Industry research reports

### Validation Criteria
- Information must be less than 6 months old
- Multiple sources must corroborate
- Official sources take precedence
- Document all discrepancies

## Expected Deliverables
- Validated/Corrected information
- Source citations
- Confidence level (High/Medium/Low)
- Expiration date of advice
```

### Brainstorming Techniques for Financial Planning

Agents can use these structured brainstorming methods to explore solutions:

1. **Financial What-If Scenarios**
   - "What if interest rates double?"
   - "What if you lose your job?"
   - "What if the market crashes 40%?"

2. **Reversal Thinking**
   - "How could you guarantee retirement failure?"
   - "What would make you go bankrupt?"
   - Then reverse for solutions

3. **First Principles Budget**
   - Strip to absolute necessities
   - Build up from survival needs
   - Add lifestyle layers consciously

4. **SCAMPER for Investments**
   - Substitute: Different asset classes
   - Combine: Multiple strategies
   - Adapt: From other successful investors
   - Modify: Risk levels
   - Put to other uses: Tax benefits
   - Eliminate: Unnecessary fees
   - Reverse: Contrarian strategies

5. **Time-Shifting Analysis**
   - "How would 1950s advice apply today?"
   - "What will matter in 2050?"
   - "If you started 10 years earlier?"

6. **Constraint-Based Planning**
   - "With only $100/month to invest?"
   - "If you could only own 3 funds?"
   - "With one year to retirement?"

## Agent Commands & Research Integration

### Universal Research Commands (Available to All Agents)

Every financial advisor agent can execute these research commands:

```yaml
research_commands:
  *research [topic]:
    description: Conduct deep research on any financial topic
    outputs:
      - Market data analysis
      - Regulatory updates
      - Competitive comparisons
      - Historical trends
      - Risk factors
    
  *validate:
    description: Verify current advice against latest data
    process:
      1. Identify claims to verify
      2. Research current sources
      3. Compare findings
      4. Update recommendations
      5. Document validation
  
  *brainstorm [challenge]:
    description: Creative problem-solving session
    techniques:
      - What-if scenarios
      - Reversal thinking
      - First principles
      - SCAMPER method
      - Constraint-based planning
  
  *market-check:
    description: Current market conditions snapshot
    includes:
      - Interest rates (Fed, mortgage, savings)
      - Market indices (S&P, NASDAQ, DOW)
      - Inflation data (CPI, PCE)
      - Currency exchange rates
      - Commodity prices
  
  *fact-check [claim]:
    description: Verify specific financial claim
    returns:
      - True/False/Partially True
      - Supporting evidence
      - Confidence level
      - Source citations
  
  *competitor-analysis [product/service]:
    description: Compare financial products
    analyzes:
      - Features comparison
      - Fee structures
      - Performance history
      - Customer reviews
      - Regulatory compliance
  
  *regulation-check [area]:
    description: Current regulatory requirements
    covers:
      - Federal regulations
      - State-specific rules
      - Recent changes
      - Upcoming changes
      - Compliance requirements
```
