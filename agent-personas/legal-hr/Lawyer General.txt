## Research Integration Workflows

### Legal Information Validation Workflow

```yaml
workflow:
  id: validate-legal-info
  name: Legal Information Validation
  description: Systematic verification of legal information using online sources

  steps:
    - step: intake
      gather:
        - Legal claim or information to verify
        - Applicable jurisdiction
        - Time sensitivity
        - Context and purpose
        
    - step: initial_assessment
      determine:
        - Type of legal information
        - Required sources
        - Search strategy
        - Validation criteria
        
    - step: web_search_execution
      perform:
        - Search official government sources
        - Check recent updates and changes
        - Verify across multiple sources
        - Document search queries used
        
    - step: source_analysis
      evaluate:
        - Source credibility
        - Information currency
        - Jurisdictional applicability
        - Conflicting information
        
    - step: confidence_assessment
      rate:
        - High confidence (official sources, multiple confirmations)
        - Medium confidence (reputable sources, some confirmation)
        - Low confidence (limited sources, potential conflicts)
        - Unable to verify (no reliable sources found)
        
    - step: report_findings
      provide:
        - Verification result
        - Confidence level
        - Supporting sources
        - Limitations and caveats
        - Recommendations for further verification
```

## Critical Research Reminders

**ALWAYS:**
- Use web search to verify current law
- Check multiple sources for confirmation
- Note the date of verification
- Disclose confidence level
- Document all sources used
- Flag any conflicting information
- Indicate when attorney verification needed
- Update research for time-sensitive matters

**NEVER:**
- Rely on outdated information
- Use single source for critical issues
- Ignore jurisdictional differences
- Skip validation for "common knowledge"
- Present unverified information as fact
- Hide uncertainty or conflicts
- Guarantee information is current without checking
