## 🔬 Research Validation Protocol

### How Research Validation Works

When any agent provides advice or creates deliverables, you can activate research validation:

1. **Command**: `*validate [topic]` - Validates current advice against online sources
2. **Command**: `*research [topic]` - Conducts deep research before providing advice
3. **Command**: `*benchmark [metric]` - Compares against industry standards
4. **Command**: `*verify-legal [requirement]` - Confirms compliance requirements

### Research Validation Process

```yaml
validation-workflow:
  step-1: Initial Assessment
    - Agent provides initial recommendation
    - Identifies claims requiring validation
    - Flags areas needing current data
    
  step-2: Research Execution
    - Searches current online sources
    - Gathers latest statistics and trends
    - Identifies best practices from top companies
    - Compiles regulatory updates
    
  step-3: Validation Analysis
    - Compares recommendations against findings
    - Identifies gaps or outdated information
    - Highlights conflicting data points
    
  step-4: Updated Recommendation
    - Revises advice based on research
    - Provides evidence citations
    - Offers confidence level (High/Medium/Low)
    - Suggests alternative approaches if needed
```

## 📝 Enhanced Agent Profiles

### research-validator - Real-Time Information Validator

**When to Use**: Before implementing any major recruitment change or strategy

**Research Capabilities**:
- Current recruitment best practices
- Industry benchmarks and standards
- Legal compliance requirements
- Technology tool comparisons
- Process effectiveness studies

**Validation Process**:
1. **Initial Review**: Analyze proposed strategy/advice
2. **Research Phase**: Search current sources (last 6 months preferred)
3. **Comparison**: Match against industry standards
4. **Report**: Provide validation score and recommendations

**Output Format**:
```markdown
## Validation Report
- **Claim/Advice**: [What was proposed]
- **Validation Status**: ✅ Confirmed / ⚠️ Partially Confirmed / ❌ Needs Revision
- **Evidence**: [Current sources and data]
- **Confidence Level**: High/Medium/Low
- **Recommendations**: [Updated advice if needed]
```

```yaml
    - Apply selected techniques interactively
    - Capture all ideas without judgment
    - Build on concepts collaboratively

    - Identify quick wins vs long-term
    - Create action plan

    - Research similar implementations
    - Verify legal/ethical compliance
    - Test with small pilot if possible
```

## 📊 Research Quality Metrics

### Validation Scoring System

```yaml
validation-metrics:
  data-currency:
    current: Less than 3 months old (Score: 100%)
    recent: 3-6 months old (Score: 80%)
    acceptable: 6-12 months old (Score: 60%)
    outdated: Over 12 months old (Score: 40%)
    
  source-credibility:
    authoritative: Government/Industry bodies (Score: 100%)
    reputable: Major firms/platforms (Score: 85%)
    general: News/blogs (Score: 70%)
    unverified: Forums/social (Score: 50%)
    
  confidence-calculation:
    high: 85-100% (Multiple current authoritative sources)
    medium: 70-84% (Some current reputable sources)
    low: Below 70% (Limited or outdated sources)
```

## 💡 Research Best Practices

### When to Research vs. Trust Experience

**Always Research**:
- Legal/compliance requirements
- Salary/compensation data
- Market-specific information
- Technology comparisons
- Competitor strategies

**Trust Experience + Validate**:
- General best practices
- Interview techniques
- Candidate experience principles
- Team management approaches
- Communication strategies

**Quick Validation Sufficient**:
- Internal processes
- Company-specific policies
- Team preferences
- Cultural fit assessments
- Soft skill evaluations
