## RESEARCH VALIDATION FRAMEWORK

### Core Research Principles

1. **Verify Before Publishing**: Every factual claim must be verifiable
2. **Current Data Only**: Statistics older than 2 years require updating
3. **Primary Sources**: Original research beats aggregated content
4. **Document Everything**: Maintain source trail for legal protection
5. **Context Matters**: Numbers without context mislead

### Research Validation Process

#### Stage 1: Claim Identification
- Extract all factual claims from copy
- Identify statistics and data points
- Flag competitor comparisons
- Mark regulatory claims
- Note market positioning statements

#### Stage 2: Verification Methods

**For Statistical Claims:**
- Locate original source
- Verify data currency (< 2 years old)
- Check methodology validity
- Confirm context accuracy
- Document source and date

**For Market Claims:**
- Verify market size (TAM/SAM/SOM)
- Validate growth rates
- Confirm segment data
- Check geographic scope
- Cross-reference multiple sources

**For Competitive Claims:**
- Verify competitor capabilities
- Validate pricing comparisons
- Confirm feature differences
- Check for recent changes
- Document comparison date

**For Product Claims:**
- Verify technical specifications
- Validate performance metrics
- Confirm compatibility statements
- Check certification claims
- Review legal compliance

#### Stage 3: Research Documentation

```markdown
## Claim Validation Report

### Claim: [Specific claim from copy]
**Source:** [Original source]
**Date:** [Publication/data date]
**Verification Status:** ✓ Verified / ⚠️ Needs Update / ✗ Cannot Verify
**Notes:** [Context or caveats]
**Risk Level:** Low / Medium / High

### Supporting Evidence:
- Primary source: [Link/citation]
- Secondary validation: [Link/citation]
- Industry standard: [Reference]
```

### Research Methodologies

#### Quick Validation (5-10 minutes)
1. Search for original source
2. Check publication date
3. Verify basic accuracy
4. Note any caveats

#### Deep Research (30-60 minutes)
1. **Define Research Objective**
   - What needs validation?
   - What decisions depend on this?
   - What's the risk of being wrong?

2. **Systematic Investigation**
   - Primary source research
   - Multiple source validation
   - Expert opinion check
   - Industry standard review

3. **Synthesis & Documentation**
   - Compile findings
   - Note contradictions
   - Document limitations
   - Provide confidence level

#### Market Research Validation
Following the market-research-template structure:

1. **Market Definition Validation**
   - Product/service category verification
   - Geographic scope confirmation
   - Segment boundary validation

2. **Size & Growth Validation**
   - TAM calculation verification
   - Growth rate cross-checking
   - Trend validation

3. **Competitive Landscape Validation**
   - Competitor identification
   - Market share verification
   - Positioning accuracy

#### Competitive Analysis Validation
Following the competitor-analysis-template:

1. **Competitor Profile Verification**
   - Company data accuracy
   - Product feature validation
   - Pricing verification
   - Market position confirmation

2. **Comparative Claims Validation**
   - Feature comparison accuracy
   - Performance metrics verification
   - Customer base validation

For complex research needs, generate structured prompts:

```markdown
## Research Objective
[What needs to be researched and why]

## Research Questions
### Must Answer:
1. [Specific, measurable question]
2. [Specific, measurable question]

### Nice to Have:
1. [Supporting question]
2. [Supporting question]

## Methodology
- Data sources: [Primary/secondary sources]
- Validation method: [Cross-reference/triangulation]
- Quality criteria: [Currency/credibility standards]

## Expected Deliverables
- Key findings with sources
- Confidence levels
- Risk assessment
- Recommendations
```

### Brainstorming for Research

When research uncovers gaps or opportunities, use structured brainstorming:

1. **Research Gap Identification**
   - What don't we know?
   - What assumptions need testing?
   - Where are competitors vulnerable?

2. **Opportunity Exploration**
   Using techniques like:
   - What-if scenarios
   - Analogical thinking
   - First principles analysis
   - Constraint exploration

3. **Validation Prioritization**
   - Immediate: Must verify before publishing
   - Important: Should verify for credibility
   - Nice-to-have: Strengthen if time allows

### Red Flags Requiring Immediate Validation

- Medical/health claims
- Financial performance promises
- Legal/regulatory statements
- Safety claims
- Comparative superiority claims
- Environmental impact statements
- "First/only/best" declarations
- Percentage improvements
- Time-based guarantees
- Price comparisons
