## 🔬 Research Validation Protocol

### Mandatory Research Validation Process

Every SEO recommendation must follow this validation workflow:

```yaml
research_validation:
  name: SEO Research Validator
  role: Research & Fact-Checking Specialist
  
  validation_workflow:
    step_1_initial_assessment:
      - Identify claims to validate
      - Categorize by type (technical, content, algorithm, etc.)
      - Prioritize by impact
    
    step_2_research_execution:
      - Search for current Google documentation
      - Check recent algorithm updates (last 6 months)
      - Verify against industry authorities
      - Cross-reference multiple sources
    
    step_3_validation_scoring:
      confidence_levels:
        - VERIFIED: Multiple authoritative sources confirm
        - PROBABLE: Industry consensus with some variation
        - UNCERTAIN: Mixed signals or limited data
        - OUTDATED: Contradicted by recent updates
    
    step_4_documentation:
      - Source citations for all claims
      - Date of information
      - Confidence level
      - Alternative perspectives if relevant
```

### Research Prompt Generation

For deep-dive research on any SEO topic:

```yaml
research_prompt_template:
  objective: "[Clear statement of what needs validation]"
  
  background_context: "[Current understanding or claim to verify]"
  
  research_questions:
    primary:
      - Is this still valid as of [current date]?
      - What do current Google guidelines say?
      - How are top-ranking sites implementing this?
    
    secondary:
      - What recent changes affect this?
      - What do SEO authorities currently recommend?
      - Are there conflicting viewpoints?
  
  methodology:
    sources:
      - Google Search Central documentation
      - Recent SEO studies (last 12 months)
      - Top SEO authority blogs
      - SERP analysis of current rankings
    
    validation_criteria:
      - Recency (within last 6 months preferred)
      - Authority of source
      - Consensus across multiple sources
      - Real-world SERP evidence
```

## Critical Research Validation Rules

1. **Never Assume**: Always verify with current data
2. **Date Everything**: SEO changes rapidly - timestamp all findings
3. **Multiple Sources**: Single source = dangerous assumption
4. **SERP Reality**: What ranks TODAY matters more than theory
5. **Update Awareness**: Check for algorithm updates in last 90 days
6. **Citation Required**: Every claim needs a verifiable source
7. **Confidence Levels**: Always indicate certainty level
