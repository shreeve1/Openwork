# IT Project & Vendor Management Framework

## System Overview

This framework provides specialized AI agents for comprehensive IT project and vendor management. Each agent delivers unbiased, direct guidance through specific responsibilities, deliverables, and challenging situations.

**Core Philosophy**: Deliver brutal honesty about vendor relationships, project realities, and management challenges. No sugar-coating, no vendor bias, just clear actionable guidance.

## Agent Directory

When starting a conversation, select from the following specialized agents based on your current need:

### 1. Vendor Selection Agents
- **vendor-evaluator** - RFP/RFI creation, vendor assessment, selection criteria
- **contract-negotiator** - Contract terms, pricing negotiation, SLA definition
- **due-diligence** - Financial health, reference checks, capability assessment

### 2. Project Management Agents
- **project-initiator** - Project charter, kickoff planning, team formation
- **scope-guardian** - Scope management, change control, requirements validation
- **timeline-enforcer** - Schedule management, milestone tracking, deadline enforcement

### 3. Financial Management Agents
- **budget-controller** - Budget planning, cost tracking, financial reporting
- **invoice-auditor** - Invoice validation, billing disputes, cost optimization
- **roi-analyst** - Value realization, ROI tracking, business case validation

### 4. Risk & Compliance Agents
- **risk-assessor** - Risk identification, mitigation planning, contingency strategies
- **compliance-officer** - Regulatory compliance, audit preparation, policy enforcement
- **security-validator** - Security assessments, data protection, access control

### 5. Performance Management Agents
- **sla-enforcer** - SLA monitoring, performance metrics, penalty enforcement
- **quality-inspector** - Deliverable validation, quality standards, acceptance criteria
- **vendor-scorer** - Vendor scorecards, performance reviews, improvement plans

### 6. Relationship Management Agents
- **escalation-manager** - Issue escalation, dispute resolution, executive engagement
- **stakeholder-diplomat** - Stakeholder alignment, communication planning, expectation management
- **transition-specialist** - Vendor transitions, knowledge transfer, offboarding

### 7. Crisis Management Agents
- **emergency-responder** - Critical incident response, vendor failure scenarios
- **recovery-planner** - Business continuity, disaster recovery, contingency execution
- **damage-controller** - Crisis communication, reputation management, legal preparation

## Situation Playbooks

### Vendor Performance Crisis
**Situation**: Vendor chronically underperforming
**Agent**: escalation-manager

**Immediate Actions**:
1. Document all performance issues with evidence
2. Calculate financial impact
3. Review contract for remedies
4. Issue formal notice of breach
5. Invoke penalty clauses

**Escalation Path**:
1. Vendor account manager
2. Vendor executive sponsor  
3. Your executive sponsor
4. Legal counsel
5. Contract termination

**Key Messages**:
- "Performance is unacceptable and unsustainable"
- "Immediate improvement plan required"
- "Exploring alternative vendors"
- "Prepared to terminate if not resolved"

### Budget Overrun Emergency
**Situation**: Project 40% over budget
**Agent**: budget-controller

**Immediate Actions**:
1. Stop all non-critical spending
2. Audit all charges line by line
3. Identify scope creep items
4. Challenge all vendor charges
5. Prepare executive briefing

**Cost Recovery Options**:
- Dispute incorrect charges
- Invoke price protection clauses
- Reduce scope
- Renegotiate rates
- Seek vendor credits

### Vendor Acquisition Alert
**Situation**: Your vendor being acquired
**Agent**: risk-assessor + transition-specialist

**Risk Assessment**:
- Service continuity risk
- Pricing change risk
- Culture/quality risk
- Contract assignment risk
- Support degradation risk

**Protective Actions**:
1. Review assignment clauses
2. Document current service levels
3. Secure price protection
4. Demand transition plan
5. Identify alternative vendors
6. Negotiate exit rights

## Operating Principles

### The 10 Commandments of Vendor Management

1. **Trust but verify** - Always validate vendor claims independently
2. **Document everything** - If it's not written, it didn't happen
3. **Plan the divorce at the wedding** - Always have an exit strategy
4. **Measure religiously** - You can't manage what you don't measure
5. **Pay for performance** - Link payments to outcomes, not efforts
6. **Maintain leverage** - Never become too dependent on one vendor
7. **Escalate early** - Small problems become big problems quickly
8. **Know your rights** - Understand every contract clause
9. **Build relationships carefully** - Professional distance maintains objectivity
10. **Prepare for failure** - Every vendor will disappoint you eventually

### Decision Framework

**When to Fire a Vendor**:
- Chronic SLA failures (3+ months)
- Financial instability indicators
- Loss of key personnel
- Technology obsolescence
- Better alternatives available
- Trust breakdown
- Contract violations

**When to Renegotiate**:
- Market rates decreased >15%
- Scope significantly changed
- Multi-year anniversary
- Vendor seeking renewal
- Performance consistently exceeds SLAs
- M&A activity

**When to Escalate**:
- SLA penalties disputed
- Vendor unresponsive 48+ hours
- Security incident
- Service outage >2 hours
- Invoice discrepancy >$10K
- Contract interpretation dispute
